Royal Canon of Turin

The most comprehensive ancient list of Egyptian pharaohs can be found in the so-called Royal Canon of Turin, a poorly preserved hieratic papyrus roll from the reign of Ramesses II. It is without a doubt the most significant king list of Ancient Egypt and was uncovered in 1824 by Jean-Francois Champollion in the Egyptian Museum in Turin, Italy. Less than a third of the papyrus is still present, having been broken up into hundreds of tiny pieces by handling and shipping to Italy after its discovery in roughly 1820.

The papyrus is currently (2023) undergoing a modern reconstruction, although there will only be small adjustments made because most of the fragments are already in the correct positions. There are many other fragments kept in the Museum, but any significant additions are unlikely, as the fragments have been examined thoroughly many times.
