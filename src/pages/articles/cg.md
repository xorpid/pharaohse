<p class="lead">
	The <em>Catalogue général des antiquités égyptiennes du Musée du Caire</em> is abbreviated <strong>CGC</strong> or <strong>CG</strong> and provides
	detailed information about objects originating in Ancient Egypt, frequently including sketches of the scenes and transcriptions of the hieroglyphs.
</p>

<p>At the end of the 19th century, the Cairo Museum finally began to take inventory of all ancient Egyptian objects. They were first grouped according to what the object was, and each group was given a block of numbers. Each of these blocks was then entered and published in the appropriate volumes. This publication was the 'Catalogue général des antiquités égyptiennes du Musée du Caire' or the <i>General catalogue of Egyptian antiquities in the Cairo Museum</i>.</p>

<p class="mb-10">
	Some of the catalogues are still in manuscript form, i.e. an unfinished collection of descriptions of the object before they were studied with a view to publication. There are marked <small>MSS</small> below. 
</p>
