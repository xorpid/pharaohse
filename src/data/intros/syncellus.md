<div class="lead">
<p>In the early ninth century, George Syncellus, a monk in Constantinople, composed a chronicle of world history which was a collection of excerpts and citations from numerous sources, including kings lists from the chronicles of <a href="/authors/africanus">Africanus</a> and <a href="/authors/eusebius">Eusebius</a>. It also had several extracts from Jewish and Christian sources.</p>
</div>

<h2 class="pt-3">The Ancient Chronicle</h2>
<p>Apparently in circulation during the time of Syncellus, it is partially based on Manetho, but a forgery created in the centuries before Syncellus.</p>
<div class="pb-5">
	<p class="pb-2">Syncellus wrote:</p>
	<blockquote>Now there circulates among the Egyptians a certain <i>Ancient Chronography</i>, on the basis of which I believe Manetho has also gone astray. It encompasses an enormous period of time consisting of 30 dynasties again in 113 generations, in (and this is not the same duration of time as Manetho) 36,525 years, treating first of <i>the Auritai</i>, then <i>the Mestraioi</i>, and third <i>the Egyptians</i>. It reads just about word for word as follows:</blockquote>
	<p class="font-bold">The reigns of the gods according to the <i>Ancient Chronicle</i></p>
	<p>There is no measured time for Hephaistos because of the fact that he shines by night and day.<br>Helios, the son of Hephaistos, reigned for <b>30,000</b> years, followed by Noble spirits for <b>184</b> years.<br>Then Kronos and all the remaining 12 gods who succeeded him reigned for <b>3,984</b> years.<br>Then the eight demigod kings for <b>217</b> years, and after them the fifteen generations of the Sothic cycle in <b>443</b> years.</p>
	<p class="px-3">After them:</p>
	<p class="px-5 tracking-wide">
	The 16<sup>th</sup> dynasty of the <i class="glows">Tanites</i>, 8 generations &mdash; <b>190</b> years<br>
	The 17<sup>th</sup> dynasty of the <i class="glows">Memphites</i>, 4 generations &mdash; <b>103</b> years<br>
	The 18<sup>th</sup> dynasty of the <i class="glows">Memphites</i>, 14 generations &mdash; <b>348</b> years<br>
	The 19<sup>th</sup> dynasty of the <i class="glows">Diospolitans</i>, 5 generations &mdash; <b>194</b> years<br>
	The 20<sup>th</sup> dynasty of the <i class="glows">Diospolitans</i>, 8 generations &mdash; <b>228</b> years<br>
	The 21<sup>st</sup> dynasty of the <i class="glows">Tanites</i>, 6 generations &mdash; <b>121</b> years<br>
	The 22<sup>nd</sup> dynasty of the <i class="glows">Tanites</i>, 3 generations &mdash; <b>48</b> years<br>
	The 23<sup>rd</sup> dynasty of the <i class="glows">Diospolitans</i>, 2 generations &mdash; <b>19</b> years<br>
	The 24<sup>th</sup> dynasty of the <i class="glows">Saites</i>, 3 generations &mdash; <b>44</b> years<br>
	The 25<sup>th</sup> dynasty of the <i class="glows">Ethiopians</i>, 3 generations &mdash; <b>44</b> years<br>
	The 26<sup>th</sup> dynasty of the <i class="glows">Memphites</i>, 7 generations &mdash; <b>177</b> years<br>
	The 27<sup>th</sup> dynasty of 5 <i class="glows">Persians</i>, 5 generations &mdash; <b>124</b> years<br>
	[ <i>The 28<sup>th</sup> dynasty of 1 <i class="glows">Saite</i>, 1 generation &mdash; 6 years</i> ]<br>
	The 29<sup>th</sup> dynasty of the <i class="glows">Tanites</i>, 7 generations &mdash; <b>39</b> years<br>
	Finally, the 30<sup>th</sup> dynasty of a single <i class="glows">Tanite</i> &mdash; <b>18</b> years<br>
	All told, 30 dynasties for a total of <b>36,525</b> years.
	</p><!-- Dyn 1-15 = 443 | Dyn 16-30 = 1703 (1697) | 443+1703= 2146 (2140) -->
	<p>Note: Actual total: 36,531. Syncellus somehow missed/left out the 28th dynasty. <br>The sum for the gods and demigods is <b>34,385</b> years, and the 29 (30) dynasties of mortal kings total <b>2,140 (2,146)</b> years. Interestingly, the vast timespan of 36,525 years is precisely 100 times the 365.25 days in a Julian year. It is also precisely 1,461 Egyptian Sothic cycles of 25 years (1,461 × 25 = 36,525).</p>
</div>

<h2 class="pt-3">The Book of Sothis</h2>
<p class="pt-2 pb-5">
	Believed by Syncellus to be genuine <a href="/authors">Manetho</a>, it was clearly based upon Eusebius and <a href="/authors/josephus">Josephus</a>, with additions that cannot easily be explained. The sequence of 86 kings is not divided into dynasties, despite Syncellus himself describing that it contained thirty. He only mentions which dynasty the kings belong to in a few cases. It is also impossible to divide the kings into dynasties as their sequence is clearly not chronological, as can be noted in the commentary.<br><br>The Book of Sothis is a pseudo-Manetho text, written by an author using Manetho’s name as a pseudonym on his own work. The quoted letter <i>To the great King Ptolemy Philadelphus Augustus</i> from Manetho is clearly a forgery and can not have been written by Manetho, as the title &quot;Augustus&quot;, originates with the Roman emperors 200 years after Manetho lived. In a best case scenario, the Book of Sothis might be sourced from a severely corrupted version of an epitome of Aegyptiaca, as there are some portions that do follow Manetho, but not in the correct order. The 86 kings are listed under the heading <i>Kings of the Egyptians</i> throughout his Chronicle.<br><br>The list of kings is included here only for the sake of completeness.
</p>
